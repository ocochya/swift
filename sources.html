Swift

I found these references incredibly useful during my creation of this website and my progress in learning Swift. Although regarded as a poor source for information, Wikipedia is generally good starting point, and is where I found out the basic information about Swift.

Next, I turned to the language's creator, Apple and found there was more that enough information on this website. Apple is eager to showcase it's program in a detailed and thorough presentation, and I found the information there to be extremely helpful. It would be my go to source for this language.

Paired with that, we have Swift.org , Swift's own website. This is had similar information to the Apple website, but it still presents it's information efficiently.

After looking at the offical sites, I found some information I found extremely useful on Macworld . They have more than enough articles on Swift, and every question I had was answered in the comments, or was covered in articles and other written pieces.

And finally, I picked up "Programming Fundamentals with Swift" by Matt Neuburg and "Swift Programming: The Big Nerd Ranch Guide" by Matthew Mathias and John Gallagher . These, in my opinion, are the most helpful books on Swift currently. I enjoyed reading them, and the information in both books is cleverly given to the reading in a manner that is easy to understand. The information isn't overwhelming, and it's simple to follow, making these two books my favourite choices.
